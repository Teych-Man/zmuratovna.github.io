📖 3. Reading Task

🌿 Text: Dealing with Stress in Everyday Life

Stress is a natural part of life. Everyone experiences stress at some point, especially students who face exams, deadlines, and responsibilities. While a small amount of stress can help people stay focused, too much stress can negatively affect mental and physical health.

There are many ways to cope with stress. Regular exercise, healthy sleep, and good time management can help reduce anxiety. Talking to friends or family members is also important. Some people practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation.

Fear is closely connected to stress. It is a natural reaction to danger, but sometimes people feel fear even when there is no real threat. Learning how to control fear and manage stress is an important life skill.
