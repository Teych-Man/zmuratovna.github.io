📖 3. Reading Task

🌍 Text: Traditional Clothing Around the World

Traditional clothing reflects the culture, history, and identity of a nation. In many countries, people wear national costumes during festivals, ceremonies, and special occasions.

For example, in Kazakhstan, traditional clothes include garments such as the chapan and saukele, which are decorated with unique patterns and symbols. In Japan, people wear kimono during cultural events. In Scotland, men wear kilts as part of their national identity.

Traditional clothes often use natural materials and reflect climate, lifestyle, and social status. Today, while modern fashion is more common, traditional clothing remains an important part of cultural heritage.
